# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective dental patients in Port Huron, Michigan and the surrounding Blue Water Area. Three overlapping audiences:

- **Parents** booking care for the whole family, often comparing 2-3 local practices on a phone after a search or word-of-mouth referral.
- **Adults with dental anxiety** who avoid the dentist and need the site itself to feel calm before they'll pick up the phone.
- **Older adults** needing restorative work (crowns, bridges, implants), who value seeing the same doctors every visit and read carefully on larger text.

Context: mobile-heavy, evaluating trust quickly. The job to be done is "decide whether this practice feels right, then call." The phone call (810-987-8310) is the only conversion; there is no online booking.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Family Dentists, PLLC — the practice of owner-dentists Dr. Jeremy Parrott and Dr. Julie Anne Hengehold at 1936 Holland Avenue, Port Huron. The site exists to convert local searches and referrals into new-patient phone calls by making the practice's defining qualities tangible: gentle, unhurried care; two hands-on owner-dentists; comfort touches (warm blankets, in-room TVs, paraffin hand treatments); practical reassurances (most insurance accepted, interest-free financing, evening & Saturday hours, same-day emergency care).

Success looks like: a visitor who lands anxious or skeptical scrolls one or two folds, feels the calm, and taps "Call to schedule." Secondary: existing patients finding hours, address, and the emergency line (810-689-4809) without friction.

## Brand Personality

**Gentle, warm, unhurried.** The voice of a practice that takes time to listen — calm confidence, never salesy. Emotional goals: ease dental anxiety, feel like a welcoming room rather than a clinic, signal modern competence without sterility. The tagline carries it: "Family dentistry that will make you smile."

## Anti-references

- **Corporate dental chains** (Aspen Dental, SmileDirect-style): stock bleached-smile photography, insurance-brochure blue, discount banners, urgency offers.
- **Clinical/sterile medical sites**: hospital white, gray sans-serif everything, portal-like density. The practice sells comfort; the site must not feel like a waiting-room form.
- **Cutesy pediatric-dentist clichés**: cartoon tooth mascots, primary-color confetti. Family-friendly is not childish.
- **Generic AI landing-page tells**: identical icon-card grids stamped without intent, gradient text, hero-metric stat blocks, template feel.

## Design Principles

1. **Comfort is the product.** Every design choice should lower the visitor's shoulders. Sell the feeling of the visit — soft light, unhurried pacing — not procedures.
2. **The phone call is the only conversion.** A tap-to-call path stays within reach from every fold (hero CTA, closing CTA, mobile action bar). Nothing competes with it.
3. **Two real doctors, not a brand.** Dr. Parrott and Dr. Hengehold are the trust anchor. Real names, real portraits (pending), first-person warmth over corporate polish.
4. **Calm over clever.** No urgency tricks, countdowns, or aggressive motion. Restraint and softness are the persuasion strategy for an anxious audience.
5. **Local and tangible.** Address, hours, map, and both phone numbers are content, not footer afterthoughts. The site should feel like it belongs to one specific building in Port Huron.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA, with an older-adult emphasis given the patient base: body text contrast ≥4.5:1 against the warm paper backgrounds, generous tap targets (the pill buttons and mobile call bar already qualify), and honored `prefers-reduced-motion` (implemented — reveals render instantly). Keyboard-accessible navigation including the mobile drawer. Scroll-reveal motion stays gentle (≤14px translate, no parallax) so it never disorients.
